BASF of Germany, one of Europe's biggest chemical makers, will buy Honeywell International's engineering plastics unit for $170 million and sell a nylon fiber business to Honeywell for $80 million. BASF will become one of the world's largest makers of engineering plastics, with about $2 billion in annual sales, a spokesman, Tim Fitzpatrick, said. The Honeywell unit reported sales of $350 million in 2001 and was profitable, he said.
