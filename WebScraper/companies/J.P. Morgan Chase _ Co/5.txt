J.P. Morgan Chase & Company and the Bank One Corporation granted each other options to buy up to 19.9 percent of their outstanding common stock in connection with their pending merger, according to a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The options granted by Bank One have an exercise price of $44.61 a share, while the options granted by J.P. Morgan have an exercise price of $38.90 a share. The holder of the option may exercise it, in whole or part, if the deal is terminated for a variety of reasons, the filing said. The companies also revealed in the filing that the value of the termination fee for the deal is $2.3 billion, which is equal to the previously reported fee of 4 percent.
