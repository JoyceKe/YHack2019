The Lockheed Martin Corporation and Affiliated Computer Services Inc. have agreed to sell separate information-technology services units to each other. Under the arrangement announced yesterday, Lockheed will buy Affiliated's federal-government technology services business for $658 million, and Affiliated will buy Lockheed's commercial information technology business for about $107 million. The transaction will allow Affiliated to concentrate on the corporate market and Lockheed to focus on civilian agencies and the Defense Department. Shares of Lockheed, which is based in Bethesda, Md., closed at $51.52, down 82 cents. Affiliated shares closed at $48.20, down $1.35.
