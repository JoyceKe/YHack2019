CHS Electronics Inc. of Miami, a microcomputer distributor, said yesterday that it had hired the investment banker Raymond James & Associates to help it explore options. The investment firm, a unit of Raymond James Financial Inc. of St. Petersburg, Fla., is reviewing CHS's operations and will recommend ways to increase the stock price. The options range from the sale of all or part of CHS to a merger or privatization, the company said. Shares of CHS, which once had a $1 billion market capitalization, have fallen more than 86 percent this year. They rose 12.5 cents yesterday, to $2.3125, giving CHS a value of $140 million. The company had 1998 sales of $8.55 billion.
