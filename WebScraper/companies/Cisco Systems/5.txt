Cisco Systems Inc., the largest maker of computer-networking equipment, will acquire the 60 percent of Allegro Systems Inc. it does not own for $109 million in stock to add network-security products. Cisco is issuing shares valued at $181 million for all of the stock of the closely held Allegro, including Cisco's own stake. Cisco bought about 40 percent of Allegro in December for an undisclosed price, said a Cisco spokeswoman, Robyn Jenkins. Allegro has developed hardware and software that Cisco will use to increase the speeds of its own products that let people connect securely over the Internet to companies' internal networks, said Ammar Hanafi, vice president for business development. Allegro is based in Milpitas, Calif. Cisco is based in San Jose, Calif.
