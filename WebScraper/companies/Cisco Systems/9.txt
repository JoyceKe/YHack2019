Cisco Systems Inc. agreed yesterday to acquire Altiga Networks Inc. and the Compatible Systems Corporation, two closely held makers of Internet access technology for corporate computer systems, for $567 million in stock. Cisco, the world's largest maker of computer-networking equipment, will swap some of its shares for all shares and options of Altiga and Compatible. Cisco, based in San Jose, Calif., did not specify how much it would pay for each company. Its stock rose 31.25 cents yesterday, to $112.3125 a share. Altiga, based in Franklin, Mass., was founded in 1998 and has 76 employees. Compatible, based in Boulder, Colo., was founded in 1988 and has 68 employees.
