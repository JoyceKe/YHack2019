Katharine Wyman Procter, a daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Arthur W. Procter Jr. of Darien, Conn., is to be married there this afternoon to William Henry Lewis, a son of Mr. and Mrs. John F. Lewis of Cleveland. The Rev. E. Tucker Bowerfind is to perform the ceremony at St. Luke's Episcopal Church.
The bride, 28, and bridegroom, 30, work in London. She is an analyst of pharmaceutical stocks for T. Rowe Price, the mutual fund company, and he is an associate in corporate finance for J. P. Morgan & Company, the investment bank.
The bride graduated from Dartmouth College and received an M.B.A. degree from Stanford University in June. Her father retired as a senior lawyer at I.B.M. in Armonk, N.Y. Her mother, Anne Procter, is an administrative assistant in Norwalk, Conn., at Northwestern Mutual Life, the insurance company.
The bridegroom graduated from Oberlin College and received J. D. and M.B.A. degrees from Case Western Reserve University, of which his father is chairman. His father is also the managing partner in Squire, Sanders & Dempsey, a Cleveland law firm. The bridegroom's mother, Cathy M. Lewis, is an owner of Resource: Careers, a Cleveland company that specializes in dual-career relocations, and she is the vice chairwoman of the Cleveland Foundation, which assists communities in northeastern Ohio.
