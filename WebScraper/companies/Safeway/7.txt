Safeway Stores, the country's largest supermarket chain, told shareholders at its annual meeting in Baltimore that it expects improvement this year in both sales and profit despite its firstquarter decline in net income. In the first quarter ended March 30, Safeway reported a 17.1 percent decline in earnings, to $16 million, or 61 cents a share, from $19.3 million, or 74 cents a share. Sales rose 10.9 percent, to $3.98 billion.
