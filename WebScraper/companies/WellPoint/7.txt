For the last two years, WellPoint, which was in the news on Monday for selling its pharmacy benefit management unit to Express Scripts for $4.7 billion, has been paying office support for Larry C. Glasscock, its nonexecutive chairman, under an agreement negotiated in December 2005.
That’s 33 percent more than the $95,000 outlined in Mr. Glasscock’s initial agreement. Under that agreement, Wellpoint will stop covering Mr. Glasscock’s office support expenses at the end of next month.
