* Publishers Information Bureau, New York, reported that magazine advertising pages in November decreased 1.1 percent from November 1995, to 22,271. For the first 11 months of 1996, total ad pages fell 1 percent, to 193,242, from the corresponding period a year earlier.
* The Women's National Basketball Association, New York, signed its initial four marketing partners. They are: Lee jeans, sold by VF Corp.; Bud Light, marketed by Anheuser-Busch Cos.; Champion, an apparel division of Sara Lee Corp., and Spalding Sports Worldwide, which is majority-owned by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co.
* Life magazine, New York, owned by Time Warner Inc., signed State Farm Insurance Cos. for a 1997 advertising program, ''Life Heroes,'' which will include a special issue of Life, advertorials and interactive media. DDB Needham Chicago, a unit of Omnicom Group, is handling the program for the insurer.
