The company, the nation's largest commercial property and casualty insurer, earned $630.7 million, or $1.33 a share, in the quarter, compared with $542.5 million, or $1.14 a share, in the quarter a year earlier. In the most recent quarter, the company had a gain of $11.3 million from investment sales, while the 1994 quarter reflected a gain of $10.2 million from investment sales.
Earnings excluding investment sales rose to $619.4 million, or $1.30 a share, from $532.3 million, or $1.12. The results matched Wall Street's expectations of $1.30 a share, according to Zacks Investment Research.
Operating profit in AIG's property and casualty business rose 20 percent, to $500.9 million, from $416.1 million a year earlier. Premiums written rose to $3.05 billion, from the year-earlier $2.82 billion.
Life insurance operating profit rose to $266.1 million from $215.1 million. Financial services operating income fell to $95.5 million from $100.3 million, primarily as a result of lower earnings in its trading group. (Bloomberg Business News)
