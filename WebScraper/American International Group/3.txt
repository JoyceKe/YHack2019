The American International Group had a record $2.71 billion profit in the fourth quarter as it earned more from life insurance and annuities and expanded in Asia. The profit of $1.03 a share was in contrast to a loss of $103.8 million, or 3 cents a share, a year earlier, when the company had $1.8 billion in one-time costs, the company said. Life insurance earnings rose 59 percent. Revenue was up 27 percent, to $81.3 billion. International earnings rose 16 percent, while earnings in the United States climbed 18 percent.
