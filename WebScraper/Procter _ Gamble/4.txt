Ilana R. Sparrow, the daughter of Miriam Lane Sparrow and Roy Sparrow of New York, was married yesterday to Benjamin A. Procter, the son of Susan Procter Dusenberry and the stepson of Philip B. Dusenberry of New York. Rabbi Michael Shevack officiated at the weekend home of the bridegroom's mother and stepfather in Sag Harbor, N.Y.
Ms. Sparrow, 30, is keeping her name. She is the manager of new media for Universal Pictures in Universal City, Calif. She graduated from New York University, where she also received an M.B.A. Her father is a professor of public management at the Robert F. Wagner School of Public Service at N.Y.U. Her mother is the general counsel for the New York Blood Center. The bride's previous marriage ended in divorce.
Mr. Procter, 26, is a digital artist at Pixel Liberation Front, a visual effects studio for movies and television in Venice, Calif. He graduated from Wesleyan University. His mother, who is retired, was a senior vice president and an associate creative director for BBDO New York, a branch of BBDO Worldwide, the advertising agency. His stepfather is the chairman of BBDO's North American unit. The bridegroom's father, the late Page S. Procter Jr., was a senior vice president and a creative director in New York at the J. Walter Thompson advertising agency.
