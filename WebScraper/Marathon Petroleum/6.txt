A growing number of American energy concerns are selling their Canadian holdings, in part because of Canada's new energy policy, which aims to increase domestic ownership of Canadian energy enterprises.
Also, Canada's new oil-pricing agreement with the provincial government of Alberta has given United States companies operating in Canada a choice of doing business there without the benefit of government incentives available to Canadian companies or disposing of their Canadian properites.
Marathon has participated in the Canadian petroleum industry for more than 30 years. Marathon Petroleum Canada is engaged in the oil and gas business. Pan Ocean Oil, acquired in 1976, owns coal, uranium and other minerals as well as oil and gas properties.
