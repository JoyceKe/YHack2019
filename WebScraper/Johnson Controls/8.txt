Johnson Controls agreed to sell a unit that hires temporary workers at military bases to IAP Worldwide Services Inc. for $260 million. The unit, World Services, has 5,000 employees and had sales of $770 million in the year that ended in September, Johnson Controls said. IAP is a government contractor owned by Cerberus Capital Management of New York. The unit being sold is part of a Johnson unit that makes, installs and runs systems that control building temperature and lighting. Johnson is based in Milwaukee.
