The Lockheed Martin Corporation said yesterday that third-quarter earnings climbed 41 percent, lifted by work developing new fighter jets. The company raised its 2004 forecast and said profit would rise next year.
Net income increased to $307 million, or 69 cents a share, from $217 million, or 48 cents, in the period a year earlier. Sales at Lockheed, which is based in Bethesda, Md., rose 4.5 percent, to $8.44 billion.
