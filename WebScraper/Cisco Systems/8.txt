U.S. Robotics Inc. and Cisco Systems Inc. said yesterday that they would develop and market computer network equipment. U.S. Robotics, a maker of modems based in Skokie, Ill., will develop devices with Cisco's technology to make it easier for separate networks to exchange data. Under the three-year deal, U.S. Robotics will make equipment for Cisco, based in San Jose, Calif. The two companies will also market each other's products. The companies said the devices they would make would replace a host of electronics, modems and routers with one device. In Nasdaq trading yesterday, shares of U.S. Robotics rose $3, to $50, and shares of Cisco gained 75 cents, to $36.625.
